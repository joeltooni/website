Joel Adebayo

MSc Electrical & Computer Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University

My research sits at the intersection of robotics and perception, driven by a deep curiosity sparked by the Moravec paradox: how humans effortlessly make sense of the world from 2D visual input, while machines still struggle. I'm particularly interested in building robots that can perceive, learn, and act autonomously, navigating and manipulating their environment in real-time, especially in dynamic and unpredictable settings.
Before CMU, I earned my B.Sc. in Systems Engineering from the University of Lagos, Nigeria. I'm passionate about exploring how intelligent robotic systems can better understand and interact with the real world, and ultimately reduce the need for extensive human oversight in complex tasks.
